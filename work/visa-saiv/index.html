The demand test that shaped a Visa launch.

Eight-week multi-market parallel smoke test of PayNest — Visa's consumer skin on its OneScore collective-credit underwriting engine. US and India ran in parallel on $5K spend. India outconverted the US 2.5x on a six-times-larger sample. Decision-grade evidence on which geography to ship.

Challenge

Visa came to us with a rare problem in B2B fintech: a working B2B capability looking for the right consumer wedge. OneScore — Visa's underwriting engine for credit-scoring collectives (families, roommates, co-founders) as single creditworthy units — already worked. The strategic question was whether to launch a consumer-facing product on top of it, in which markets, and against which audience. Nine months of prior qualitative research had narrowed the candidate audience to three personas. The decision was a launch-readiness call: validate consumer demand for PayNest in two candidate markets simultaneously, in eight weeks, with decision-grade evidence on which to ship.

Solution

  • Multi-market parallel smoke test. Dedicated landing pages on separate domains per market — saiv.app for the US, saiv.in for India.
  • Four weekly experiment sprints across Facebook, Instagram, and Google AdWords with a $5,000 ad budget split between markets.
  • Google Analytics instrumentation layer capturing conversion behavior by demographic, affinity category, and in-market intent segment.
  • Binary kill-or-commit question at the market level: which geography deserves a real launch, and against which audience cut.

Results

  • India outconverted the US by approximately 2.5x on a six-times-larger sample.
  • Hottest in-market segments concentrated in Personal Loans, Mortgage, Business Loans, and Credit Cards — converting at 3–4x the all-visitor rate.
  • Converting audience profile — younger adults (18–34, male-skewed) in metro India shopping actively for credit.
  • Decision-grade evidence on which geography to ship and which audience cut to lead with.

Selected experiments.

Sequencing model

Two-market parallel smoke test

Synthesis from market 1, translate landing page and creative for market 2, run the second demand test, run the comparison synthesis. The sequencing model is reusable for any "which market first" launch question.

Audience surfacing

In-market intent segmentation

GA's in-market and affinity segments surfaced the converting audience profile by category, not just demographics — Personal Loans, Mortgage, Business Loans, and Credit Cards segments converting at 3–4x the all-visitor rate.

Constrained budget

$5K paid budget for a launch-readiness call

A four-sprint paid program at $5K total ad spend produced statistically defensible signal at the market level. The constraint forced sharper hypothesis design — not "test everything" but "find the one signal that decides the launch."

Architecture

Per-market domain isolation

saiv.app for the US, saiv.in for India — separate domains, separate analytics property, separate audience cuts. The architecture isolated market-specific signal from cross-market noise.

What this engagement says about multi-market launch validation.

Plan a full quarter between markets at minimum. The synthesis-translate-test-compare loop produces clean comparative signal when run in sequence; running both markets simultaneously is a budget-and-attention dilution that this engagement specifically avoided. The Visa SAIV pattern is the canonical reference for any "which market first" question on the validation roadmap.
